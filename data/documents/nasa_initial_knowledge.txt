NASA Mission Database - Initial Knowledge Base

MISSION LAUNCH DATES:
Voyager 1 was launched on September 5, 1977 from Kennedy Space Center in Florida.
Voyager 2 was launched on August 20, 1977 from Kennedy Space Center in Florida.
Mars Curiosity rover was launched on November 26, 2011 from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station.
Perseverance rover was launched on July 30, 2020 from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station.
Hubble Space Telescope was launched on April 24, 1990 aboard Space Shuttle Discovery.
James Webb Space Telescope was launched on December 25, 2021 aboard Ariane 5 rocket.

MISSION TECHNOLOGIES:
Voyager 1 uses ion propulsion technology for deep space navigation.
Mars Curiosity uses nuclear power technology for surface operations.
Voyager 1 uses radioisotope thermoelectric generators for power.
Mars Curiosity uses nuclear power for its scientific instruments.

PLANETARY EXPLORATION:
Voyager 1 studied Jupiter during its flyby mission in 1979.
Voyager 1 studied Saturn during its flyby mission in 1980.
Mars Curiosity studies Mars through surface exploration and scientific analysis.
Perseverance studies Mars through surface exploration and sample collection.

MISSION STATUS:
Voyager 1 is currently active and continues to transmit data from interstellar space.
Voyager 2 is currently active and continues to transmit data from interstellar space.
Mars Curiosity is currently active and continues to explore the surface of Mars.
Perseverance is currently active and continues to explore the surface of Mars.
Hubble Space Telescope is currently active and continues to observe the universe.
James Webb Space Telescope is currently active and continues to observe the universe.

MISSION OBJECTIVES:
Voyager 1's primary objective is to explore the outer planets and interstellar space.
Voyager 2's primary objective is to explore the outer planets and interstellar space.
Mars Curiosity's primary objective is to determine if Mars could have supported microbial life.
Perseverance's primary objective is to search for signs of ancient life and collect samples.
Hubble Space Telescope's primary objective is to observe the universe in visible, ultraviolet, and near-infrared light.
James Webb Space Telescope's primary objective is to observe the universe in infrared light.
